Deployments

Every push to a connected repository creates a deployment — an immutable, independently-addressable build of your app that you can preview, promote, or roll back.

How a deploy works

When you push, Atlas detects your framework, runs the build in an isolated container, and publishes the output to the global edge. The deploy gets a unique URL immediately; promoting it to production is a separate, instant step — your build is never coupled to a release.

# deploy the current directory
atlas deploy --prod
# → https://atlas-web-7f3a.atlas.app
Note — Preview deploys are kept for 30 days and are safe to share; each is sandboxed with its own environment variables.

Promoting to production

Promote either by merging to your production branch or with a single command. Promotion swaps the production alias atomically, so users never see a half-deployed state.

Caution — Environment variables scoped to production are not available in preview builds. Set shared values at the project level.

The quiet drift of the size-8 garment

By Maren Holt · June 26, 2026 · 9 min read
— lead photograph —

A garment labelled "size 8" has quietly grown for decades. The number on the tag stayed put; the tape measure did not. The story of that drift is, in miniature, the story of how a standard erodes when no one is accountable for it.

It begins, as these things often do, with a well-meaning compromise — and ends with a number that means almost nothing at all.

"The tag is a promise. Somewhere along the way, the promise stopped being kept."

How a standard erodes

Each individual adjustment was defensible. A label nudged half a centimetre to flatter; a competitor matched; a decade passed. No single decision broke the standard — the absence of anyone defending it did.

What's left is a measurement that drifts with fashion and confidence rather than the body it claims to describe. The fix isn't a new number. It's someone whose job is to hold the old one still.
