On Restraint · IV
Composition № 11

Set for sustained reading, the letterform has a single duty: to disappear. Moderate contrast, organic proportions, an even colour down the whole page — nothing that makes you notice the type instead of the words.

This is the far pole from display. Where Scher makes type perform, the reading face recedes; restraint is the entire craft. The measure of success is plain — you should finish the paragraph without once having seen the font.

Type that recedes

After Garamond — legibility as even, unnoticed texture.
