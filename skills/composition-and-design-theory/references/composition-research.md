# Graphic Design — Composition & Theory: Research Synthesis

**Purpose.** Raw material for a new `design-craft` skill on **composition and design theory** —
medium-agnostic (must read as fine art and print, *not* as a webpage), built to *learn from the
masters, extract the transferable parts, and invent an original design language*. Feeds
[[developing-style]].

**Method.** Two layers:
1. A deep-research workflow — 112 agents, ~3.8M tokens, 6 angles, 29 sources fetched, 139 claims
   extracted, top 25 adversarially verified (3-vote, need 2/3 to kill) → **23 confirmed, 2 killed**.
   Strong on cross-cutting theory; thin on per-master coverage (only Wyman + Kidd survived its budget).
2. Five targeted per-master research passes filling that gap (Vignelli/Sutnar/Nitsche/Beall/Lissitzky;
   Garamond/Pineles/Scher; Saville/Chwast/Kidd; Dixon/Fuerte/Trabucco-Campos/"James Martin"/Maeda;
   the AI-image frontier). These are single-pass, source-rated, not 3-vote verified.

**Snapshot date:** 2026-06-29. AI-image findings rest on Jan-2026 benchmarks — re-check before quoting numbers.

## How to read the labels

- **[STRONG]** — peer-reviewed study or normative standard. Binding; don't relax on a whim.
- **[CONVENTION]** — sound practice from a named authority / the design canon; thin or no controlled evidence.
  The default starting point; deviate with reason.
- **[MYTH]** — the popular claim failed verification. Do **not** codify.
- Source quality: **P** primary (designer's own words, their book, a museum essay) · **C** serious
  criticism (Eye, Artforum, Typotheque, AIGA, peer-reviewed) · **W** weak (SEO/blog/vendor-marketing).

## Part 1 — Cross-cutting fundamentals (verified)

These are the spine. All [STRONG] unless noted, all medium-agnostic.

### 1.1 The grid is a universal ordering system, not a web device — [STRONG]
The grid divides a 2D plane (**or a 3D space**) into fields so *all* elements — type, photography,
illustration, colour — are disposed within one rational framework. Müller-Brockmann frames it not as
a convenience but as a *stated philosophy*: "submitting to laws of universal validity… the will to
systematize, to clarify… objectivity instead of subjectivity." His own caveat: the grid is "an aid,
not a guarantee" (it governs order, not quality). The 3D clause and the book's address to
"three-dimensional designers" make the medium-agnostic reading **sourced, not inferred**.
*Source: Müller-Brockmann, Grid Systems in Graphic Design (1981), P, verified verbatim across 3 mirrors.*

### 1.2 Perceptual grouping is a named, OPEN toolkit — [STRONG]
Separate marks bind into a perceived unit via named factors: classical (proximity, similarity, common
fate, good continuation, closure, symmetry, parallelism) plus newer (synchrony, common region, element
& uniform connectedness). **Caveat to respect:** the set is *open and evolving* — the source uses
"e.g." and criticizes old Gestalt's "proliferation of laws." Present as an open toolkit of grouping
cues; drop "finite/closed/exactly the levers."
*Source: Wagemans et al. 2012, "A Century of Gestalt Psychology I," Psychological Bulletin 138(6), P.*

### 1.3 Figure-ground and hierarchy can be engineered (biased, not forced) — [STRONG]
Which region reads as *figure* vs *ground* is governed by measurable cues — convexity, symmetry, small
relative size, surroundedness, lower region, top-bottom polarity. So a layout can **reliably bias** what
comes forward and what recedes, and a coherent form tends to organize parts around an inner center to
which others relate hierarchically. **Two caveats:** (1) cues are *probabilistic biases*, not deterministic
switches — familiarity/attention/meaning can compete; read "engineered" as "reliably biased." (2) The leap
from "perceptual fact" to "design rule" is convention, not the source's claim.
*Source: Wagemans et al. 2012 (Sec 2.2, 5.2–5.3); corroborated Vecera et al. 2002. P.*

### 1.4 Design the layout as one field, not a parts list — [STRONG perception / CONVENTION bridge]
Two perceptual facts justify treating composition as unified: **Prägnanz** (perception resolves a field
into the simplest, most encompassing structure the input allows) and **non-additivity** (a perceived
whole is *different from* — not "greater than" — the sum of parts). **Caveats:** Prägnanz is criticized
as circular (Köhler himself was "embarrassed"); present it as a *description of what perception does*,
not a mechanism. The bridge "this is why simplification feels right" is a mainstream design
interpretation, not a sourced empirical finding. Don't cite Köhler's brain-field theory as live science.
*Source: Wagemans et al. 2012, verbatim Wertheimer 1923. P.*

### 1.5 Meaning is a checkable system: read it, classify it, build it from syntax — [STRONG]
- **Two reading levels:** denotative (literal) and connotative (culturally coded) — meaning extends far
  beyond what is literally depicted (Barthes, "Rhetoric of the Image," 1964).
- **Sign types (Peirce):** icon (resemblance), index (relation/pointing), symbol (convention); one image
  can be all three (a photo is an "indexical icon").
- **Syntax produces meaning:** a visual text is read holistically, but *how elements are joined*
  determines meaning — three lines read as a triangle, an "H," or a picnic table. **Arrangement, not the
  parts list, makes the meaning.** (This overlaps with and reinforces 1.4.)
*Sources: Danesi, "Visual Rhetoric and Semiotic" (Oxford RE Communication), P; Stanford Encyclopedia
(Peirce). Barthes later complicated the clean denotation/connotation split — refines, doesn't refute.*

### 1.6 Design is theory-teachable — premise, not proof — [CONVENTION / MEDIUM]
Design is distinct from fine art and can be approached systematically via theory rather than innate
talent — legitimizing the whole "extract transferable principles" project. **But** the only source is a
weak journal, rescued by the mainstream design-education canon (Bauhaus, HfG Ulm, the 1960s Design
Methods Movement, Simon's *Sciences of the Artificial*). **Critical honesty:** a separate N=30 experiment
claiming that teaching semiotics measurably *improves* composition scores was **REFUTED 0-3**. So: design
is theory-teachable *in principle*; there is **no verified proof that any specific theory improves output
quality**. The skill's premise rests on plausibility + canon, not on proof. Say so.

## Part 2 — Per-master exemplars (the transferable moves)

Format: **contribution → transferable rule (vs personal style) → medium check → source/confidence.**
Grouped by what each one proves. Master-level claims are **[CONVENTION]** unless tied to a [STRONG] item
above; they answer "what did this designer do/argue," not "what is empirically true of perception."

### Group A — Constructed space & dynamism (the formal/expressive pole)

**El Lissitzky** — *the most medium-agnostic of all; fine art ↔ architecture ↔ type was his explicit project.*
- Contribution: the surface is **constructed space**, not a flat thing to decorate (Proun = "interchange
  station from painting to architecture"). Type included: "words… are taken in by seeing, not by hearing."
- Transferable rule: **diagonal = dynamic, right angle = static** (he stated it as theory); use asymmetry
  for energy; make forms **tangent to the frame** so the edge is active; treat the page as architected space.
- Personal style: the Suprematist/Constructivist vocabulary and revolutionary palette.
- Source: Artforum (C, with his quotes); "Topography of Typography" 1923 (P, exact wording 2nd-hand); Monoskop archive (P).

**Lester Beall** — *formal rules agnostic; the arrow-as-instruction device is communication-bound.*
- Contribution: composition as **engineered movement and tension** — "spurn symmetry," make the page
  "alive with movement," design for an *emotional reaction*. "The first requirement of a designer is thought."
- Transferable rule: **thrust and counter-thrust** (angled photos vs reversed lines); **directional cues
  (arrows, angled forms)** to route the eye; flat colour planes + silhouetted photos; reduce to bold symbols.
- Personal style: Depression-era Americana imagery, the REA palette/iconography.
- Source: MoMA essay (P-grade); RIT Cary Collection (C); direct quotes 2nd-hand (AIGA essay unreachable).

**Erik Nitsche** — *abstraction agnostic; cinematic pacing is sequence-bound.*
- Contribution: communicate through **abstraction to a timeless symbol**, rejecting both literalism and
  cold Swiss orthodoxy ("a little too cold for our uses").
- Transferable rule: distil a subject to its essential symbol; design for timelessness; **compose
  *sequences* with deliberate pacing** ("evolve from sea to sky through the turning of pages"), not just
  single frames; pair type for warmth/contrast (sans + classical serif).
- Personal style: the Klee-ish mark-making, the General Dynamics look.
- Source: Typotheque, "The Reluctant Modernist" (C, with his own quotes) — strongest single source in Group A.

### Group B — Systems, flow & reduction (the rational pole)

**Massimo Vignelli** — *communication-bound (presumes a message + audience); not a fine-art theory.*
- Contribution: design is a discipline, not self-expression. Every brief: **semantics** (find the meaning)
  → **syntactics** (structure it: grid, type, hierarchy) → **pragmatics** (if it isn't understood it's
  worthless). Goal = "intellectual elegance," timelessness not trendiness.
- Transferable rule: start from meaning before form; grid for consistency; **constrain the type palette
  hard** (he ran an agency's output on *four* faces — "it's not the type, but what you do with it"); test
  for comprehension not cleverness.
- Personal style: the austere Helvetica/Swiss look; *his* particular faces.
- Source: *The Vignelli Canon* (his own book, free PDF, P); Fonts In Use (C); TDC (C).

**Ladislav Sutnar** — *information/communication-bound; a founder of information design.*
- Contribution: "good design implies **control of visual flow**" — information *engineered into a guided
  path* the eye follows, via "simplification and coordination of design factors."
- Transferable rule: treat information as a **designed flow**; his seven principles of Controlled Visual
  Flow (upper-right importance, starts/stops, active empty space, visual dominance, visual interest,
  coordination, integration) are near-checklist-portable; white space and rules are *routing devices*.
- Personal style: the mid-century geometric/modernist look.
- Source: Sutnar, *Controlled Visual Flow* (1943, P) — **but the 7-principle list is confirmed 2nd-hand only**
  (primary book not read; seed source 503'd). Treat list as confirmed-but-second-hand.

**John Maeda** — *split him: the Laws are medium-agnostic; computational design is screen/generative-bound.*
- Contribution (two): **computational design** (design authored through code; medium = process) and
  **simplicity as deliberate reduction** ("subtracting the obvious, adding the meaningful").
- Transferable rule: **Reduce → Organize** ("organize makes a system of many appear fewer" — a direct
  grouping/layout rule); treat simplicity ↔ complexity as a managed balance; "shrink, hide, embody."
- Personal style: the generative/code-art aesthetic of his Media Lab work.
- Source: *The Laws of Simplicity* (MIT Press, P); lawsofsimplicity.com (P). **Flag:** computational design
  is code/screen-native — keep it from quietly steering the skill toward web-looking output.

**Simon Dixon / DixonBaxi** — *principle agnostic; motion + sonic layers are time/screen-bound.*
- Contribution: identity is a **behavioural system**, not a logo — "a logo is an empty vessel… identity is
  only as powerful as the meaning you fill it with." "Intelligent Identities": fixed *and* flexed at once.
- Transferable rule: **design the rules/behaviours** (flexible system + fixed through-line) rather than one
  locked composition; stated strategic principles govern every element.
- Personal style / caveat: the loud kinetic streaming aesthetic; **motion + sonic systems are time-based and
  do not transfer to a static page.** Use the systems-thinking, not the assumption that every brand needs motion.
- Source: justcreative.com capturing Dixon's quotes (C/P); dixonbaxi.com (P, thin scrape). Seed 403'd.

*(Wolff Olins — generative/"living" identity, a rule-set generating many context-specific expressions —
belongs here too as the institutional exponent. **Strongly motion + screen native today; flag as the most
screen-leaning item.** See Part 6 on the "James Martin" non-verification.)*

### Group C — Type from recede → perform (the type-as-meaning axis)

A clean spectrum the skill should teach as **one axis**: the typeface and its placement are *semantic
decisions, not neutral defaults* — and the right choice depends on whether the job is to **recede (text)**
or **perform (display)**.

**Claude Garamond** — *the recede pole; body-text bound.*
- Contribution: the humanist old-style ("garalde") roman as the model of **text-legible** letterform —
  moderate contrast, organic proportions, even "colour" on the page.
- Transferable rule: for sustained reading the letterform should **disappear into even texture** —
  legibility through restraint and evenness, not novelty. (Complementary *opposite* of Scher.)
- Medium check: body-text bound; does **not** transfer to poster/display logic (where disruption is the point).
- **[MYTH] flag (confirmed true):** most fonts named "Garamond" are **not** based on Claude Garamond —
  Beatrice Warde (1926) showed the credited punches were cut by **Jean Jannon c. 1615–21**. Monotype/ATF/ITC
  Garamond derive from Jannon; genuine Garamond revivals = Stempel Garamond, Sabon; Adobe Garamond uses
  Garamond romans + Granjon italics. **Cite the *model* ("old-style roman in the Garamond tradition"), not the man.**
- Source: Typofonderie Gazette (C); Wikipedia carrying the Warde correction (reference-grade).

**Cipe Pineles** — *the integration middle; editorial-sequence arc is paginated-bound.*
- Contribution: editorial art direction as an **authorial** discipline — type + image composed into one
  narrative system across a *sequence* of spreads; first to commission **fine artists** for mass-market editorial.
- Transferable rule: treat type and image as **one integrated system across a sequence**; raise the imagery's
  quality ceiling by commissioning and briefing *for intent, not output*; let a theme structure a multi-page arc.
  Also: **letterform-as-object / visual pun** (object substitutes for a letter) — this part transfers to posters/identity.
- Medium check: the integration principle generalizes (decks, campaigns, multi-screen flows); the
  cover-states-theme → 14-pages-develop-it *arc* assumes pagination.
- Source: Eye, "The Tenth Pioneer" (C, most concrete); AIGA Eye on Design (C). AIGA medalist essay 403'd (unverified).

**Paula Scher** — *the perform pole; strongly medium-agnostic incl. fine art.*
- Contribution: **"type is image"** — typography *performs* the subject and carries emotion; rejects
  neutrality ("Helvetica neutralises feeling").
- Transferable rule: type can be the **primary image and the whole composition**; typeface choice is a
  semantic/emotional decision; **activate the whole frame** — edge-to-edge density, type running off edges
  ("no white space," the NYC wheat-paste field); scale type up to architectural.
- Personal style: the maximalist density, the hand-painted typographic *maps*, the Constructivist-meets-pop look.
- Medium check: holds across poster/identity/signage/environment **and fine art** (the maps prove it). Failure
  mode: anti-pattern for sustained body reading (Garamond's domain).
- Source: Eye "Reputations" (P, her words); Pentagram (P); PRINT (C). "Serious play" exact phrase lightly unverified
  (Pentagram uses "serious, not solemn").

**Andrea Trabucco-Campos** — *principle agnostic; his output is a provocation, not functional type design.*
- Contribution: letterforms are **cultural / art-historical containers**, not neutral tools — type
  accumulates meaning through how it's been used; reuse is a deliberate citation.
- Transferable rule: choose/treat type with awareness of its **historical lineage**; know design history
  well enough to "find the right match." (Pairs with semiotics §1.5 and Saville's appropriation.)
- Personal style: the AI-generated art-history-mashup register (*Artificial Typography*: "what if Noguchi
  sculpted the letter N?"). AI shifted his role "from creator to curator and provoker through language."
- Source: Pentagram (P); Readymag (P); It's Nice That (C). **Flag:** seed's "Type02" is a conflation — the
  book is *Artificial Typography*; "Type02" unsourced.

### Group D — First impression, meaning-by-placement & generative rule

**Lance Wyman** — *Group D's anchor. The generative-system lesson is verified; the popular "single rule" origin story is NOT.*
- Contribution: a **generative system fused with culture**. Mexico '68 propagated one repeating
  parallel-line motif across the numerals "68", the word MEXICO, and the whole identity — one mark encoding
  event, place, year.
- **Correction [V27 — REFUTED, high]:** the radiating parallel lines were **Huichol / pre-Hispanic-derived,
  NOT extended from the circular five-ring Olympic logo**, and the motif drew on **three influences (Huichol
  yarn-painting + 1960s Op-art + the Olympic context), not one compositional rule.** The tidy "one rule from
  the five-ring logo" story is a simplification the research killed — don't repeat it.
- Transferable rule (this part holds): **derive an identity from one structural motif, then bind that motif to
  culturally resonant style** — distinct from copying the style itself.
- Source: Walker Art Center (designer's own first-person account, P), corroborated V&A, Cooper Hewitt. The
  generative-system principle was upheld 3-vote; the single-rule / five-ring specifics were refuted (V27).

**Peter Saville** — *medium-agnostic (appropriation is a fine-art lineage: Duchamp, Warhol).*
- Contribution: **recontextualization / appropriation as the composing act** — meaning is manufactured by
  selecting, not drawing: lift a charged found image out of its origin and drop it into a new context.
- Transferable rule: **meaning can come from placement, not creation**; curate a found image and let the
  context-gap do the work; **subtract identifying text** so the borrowed image carries the message
  ("type omission to intensify the quotation"); **productive dissonance** (sedate image vs unsettling music).
- Personal style: the romantic-modernist Factory register.
- Source: Design Museum (P-adjacent); V&A (P); Wikipedia quoting critic Alice Twemlow (C).
  **Correction:** the Eye "Peter Saville Principle" URL does **not** argue appropriation (it's his "covers
  aren't graphic design" provocation) — don't cite it for this claim.

**Seymour Chwast (Push Pin)** — *principle agnostic; his flat-illustration execution is print/poster-leaning.*
- Contribution: **strategic eclectic revivalism** — against Swiss orthodoxy, reach back to historical/
  vernacular styles (Victorian, Art Nouveau, Deco, woodcut) as a live toolbox; reinstate ornament, narrative, wit.
- Transferable rule: **match the visual register to the message, and position deliberately *against* the
  prevailing orthodoxy.** Style pluralism is a strategy, not indiscipline. (This directly prefigures
  [[developing-style]]'s "distinctiveness = deviation from convention.")
- Personal style: the Push Pin flat-line comic sensibility.
- Source: Design Observer (C); Wikipedia (Push Pin); "style-per-assignment" claim lightly sourced (archive 403'd).

**Chip Kidd** — *medium-agnostic; verified in the deep-research pass (medium confidence, single talk).*
- Contribution: two levers of instant communication — **clarity vs mystery** — and knowing which the
  content demands. "Clarity gets to the point… blunt, honest." "Mystery demands to be decoded, and when
  it's done right, we really, really want to."
- Transferable rule: **decide up front whether the piece must be *recognized* (lead with clarity) or
  *investigated* (use mystery — withhold, encode, delay the payoff); rarely both at full volume.** Then
  **distil to one idea** ("a cover is a haiku of the story"). Jurassic Park = clarity-to-icon.
- Source: TED talk (P, transcript JS-rendered, quotes 2nd-hand-relayed); artnet (Jurassic Park origin, C).

### Group E — Generative geometric vocabulary (the cleanest print-safe modern move)

**Verónica Fuerte / Hey Studio** — *fully medium-agnostic; Bauhaus/Swiss lineage, not screen-bound.*
- Contribution: geometry + colour as a **universal language** — complex ideas distilled to simple geometric
  forms; "don't overcomplicate… the idea should be powerful and clear"; the brand should still "feel like a person."
- Transferable rule: **constrain to a small geometric vocabulary (circles, triangles, arcs) + a flat palette,
  then generate everything from it** — consistency and recognizability fall out of the constraint; warmth
  comes from concept and colour, not from loosening the geometry.
- Personal style: the cheerful rounded Mediterranean-bright look.
- Source: designboom (P); designwanted (P); Creative Bloq (C). One of the cleanest principles for a print-aware skill.

## Part 3 — The AI-image frontier (so the language doesn't date)

### 3.1 Who is actually acclaimed (establishment-recognized)
- **Refik Anadol** — canonical case. *Unsupervised* (2022) at MoMA, trained on ~178k-work collection
  metadata, **acquired into MoMA's permanent collection**. Method: curates large datasets, **trains custom
  models from scratch**, navigates latent space into "AI data sculptures." (MoMA P; Artforum C.)
- **Mario Klingemann** — longest pedigree; Lumen Gold, Ars Electronica; Pompidou/Barbican/Met. GAN latent-
  space morphing, "process over finished product." (Wikipedia C; Goethe-Institut P.)
- **Anna Ridler** — conceptually cleanest. *Myriad (Tulips)*: ~10,000 **hand-photographed, hand-labeled**
  images — **the dataset itself is the artwork**, exposing hidden human labor. (Wikipedia C; Barbican C.)
- **Holly Herndon & Mat Dryhurst** — *xhairymutantx*, **2024 Whitney Biennial**; interrogates how a named
  person is represented on the AI substrate. (Whitney P; Artnet/ArtReview C.)
- **Trevor Paglen / Jake Elwes** — machine-vision *critique* more than image-composition. (Paglen: 2026 LG
  Guggenheim Award.)
- **Sougwen Chung** — robotic-drawing *performance*, not text-to-image (live human-robot feedback). (MIT Tech Review C.)

### 3.2 What critics actually praise — concept/process, NOT classical composition
The dominant pattern: **acclaim attaches to the concept, dataset, and process; the image/composition is
rarely the cited virtue — and when judged on its own it gets attacked.** Anadol is the test case:
- Praised (concept): art-historical automatism (Kuo); "images that could have been made but never were" (Reas);
  data-as-material, latent-space navigation, emergent form, **scale and immersion**.
- Attacked (image as composition): Jerry Saltz — *"a banal screensaver… mediocre spectacle"*; Ben Davis —
  *"an extremely intelligent lava lamp."*

> **Takeaway for the skill.** In the serious AI-art world, "good composition" in the classical sense is
> almost never what earns praise. What earns it: the **idea, the dataset, the process**, and formally
> **scale, immersion, latent-space *motion*, emergent form**. Decorative-looking output is the standard
> line of attack. This is the *opposite* failure mode from generic webby AI output — and a lever the
> original language can exploit: **concept + process as the content, composition imposed by a human.**

### 3.3 Compositional control — the practitioner ladder (most → least control)
Raw text-to-image is **documented-weak** at spatial composition (root causes: spatial language under-
represented in LAION/COCO-class training data; CLIP text-encoder limits — CoMPaSS arXiv 2412.13195;
CompAlign 2505.11178; "Getting it Right" 2404.01197 — all C/primary).
1. **Human-directed compositing pipeline** (most control) — AI makes layers/elements; the human composes
   them in Photoshop/AE/Nuke. Control is total because the human does the composing.
2. **ControlNet** (strong structural) — condition on edge/depth/segmentation/pose maps; hand it the layout,
   it fills style. The primary mechanism for *forcing* spatial structure. (Zhang et al., ICCV 2023, primary.)
3. **Inpainting / outpainting** (strong local) — mask + regenerate/extend.
4. **img2img** (medium) — seed from an existing image to inherit layout.
5. **Regional prompting** (partial) — different prompts per region.
6. **Custom-trained model** — controls **vocabulary/style, NOT layout**. (Anadol/Ridler/Herndon all do this.)
7. **Raw prompting** — little-to-no spatial-layout control.

### 3.4 Classical principles: carry over vs break (reasoned inference, not a single cited source)
- **Carry over** (training images already obey them, so output gets them "for free"): figure-ground
  separation, atmospheric depth, lighting coherence, loose rule-of-thirds framing.
- **Break — must be imposed by the human**: grid & precise layout; deliberate hierarchy across 3+ elements;
  intentional eye-path/reading order; exact spatial relations/counting/attribute binding; type placement &
  negative space as deliberate structure.
- **Dating flag:** the "raw T2I is weak at composition" premise is the most likely to date — the 2025–26
  papers exist *to fix* it; state it as "true of base models, narrowing fast," not permanent.

## Part 4 — Myths (do NOT codify)

- **[MYTH] Weight / center-of-mass theory of balance** (Arnheim after Ross). Tested across five studies,
  detailed results did not support it; viewers framed images identically regardless of disk size/greyness/
  background. **Calibration:** *unproven*, not "all balance is a myth" — residual balance effects remain
  unexplained. (McManus, Stöver & Kim 2011, i-Perception, P.)
- **[MYTH] Golden-ratio "sacred geometry."** The Parthenon was not designed on φ (overlays fit only by
  including empty air); Mona Lisa / Vitruvian Man attributions are baseless (Leonardo's text cites only
  whole-number ratios). The only contradicting sources sell golden-ratio software. **Don't justify
  composition by φ.** (PMC10792139, P; Markowsky 1992; Livio 2002.)
- **[MYTH-buster] "Garamond" fonts** — see §Garamond: cite the *model*, not the man.
- **[REFUTED] "Teaching semiotics measurably improves composition scores"** — N=30 study failed 0-3. No
  verified proof any theory improves output *quality* (only that design is teachable in principle).
- **[W / hype] "AI applies the rule of thirds / golden triangle for you"** — SEO/vendor claims; the eye-path
  and layout do *not* emerge reliably (see §3.4).

## Part 5 — The through-lines (toward an original, ownable language)

These recur across *independent* masters — the strongest candidates for the skill's spine and for the
"invent our own language" goal. Each is medium-agnostic.

1. **Constrain to a small generative vocabulary, then derive everything from it.** Wyman (one geometric
   rule → whole identity), Fuerte (geometric primitives kit), Vignelli (a handful of faces forever), Maeda
   (reduce; organize many into few). *The single most repeated transferable move.*
2. **Diagonal/asymmetry = dynamism; orthogonal = static.** Lissitzky stated it; Beall and Nitsche applied it.
3. **Control the eye's path by reduction + explicit routing.** Sutnar's flow, Beall's arrows, Vignelli's
   grid, Maeda's organize, Nitsche's pacing.
4. **Type is semantic, never neutral — on a recede↔perform axis.** Garamond (recede/text) ↔ Pineles
   (integrate) ↔ Scher (perform/image) ↔ Trabucco-Campos (type carries history).
5. **Meaning comes from arrangement / placement / context, not the inventory.** Semiotics §1.5 (syntax),
   Saville (appropriation), Peirce (sign relations), Gestalt non-additivity §1.4.
6. **Design the system/rules, not the single artifact.** Dixon ("empty vessel"), Wolff Olins (generative
   identity), Maeda ("organize"). *(Carry the motion/screen-bound caveat.)*
7. **Choose the encoding: clarity vs mystery.** Kidd. Then distil to one idea.
8. **Position deliberately against the prevailing convention.** Chwast (anti-Swiss revivalism); = the core
   of [[developing-style]] ("distinctiveness is deviation from convention").
9. **Compose as one unified field, not a parts list.** Gestalt §1.4 + §1.2; everyone in Group A/B in practice.
10. **Treat the frame/edge as active, and the surface as constructed space.** Lissitzky (tangency), Scher
    (edge-to-edge fill).

**The seam between #6 and the medium-agnostic mandate is the one real tension** — the most modern,
acclaimed identity work (Dixon, Wolff Olins, Maeda's computational design) is motion + screen native, and
the brief explicitly says *don't look like a webpage*. The skill must take the systems-thinking from these
without inheriting their motion/screen assumptions. Flagged throughout Part 2.

## Part 6 — Open questions & gaps

1. **What positively predicts perceived balance?** The weight/axes model is unproven yet residual balance
   effects exist (McManus: significant results "requiring explanation"). Can the language encode balance as
   a rule, or only as a feel? *Unresolved by the evidence.*
2. **Acclaimed-AI *workarounds* in practice** — the research established the spatial-composition *weakness*
   and the control *ladder* (§3.3), but not a deep account of how the best practitioners actually marry
   classical composition with generative tools on real projects. *(Will be picked up in the tooling pass.)*
