# Meridian — The Credible Default

**Identity (one line):** Clean, credible corporate — an off-white page, one reserved blue, hairline rules, generous air; it reads *solid and professional* and then gets out of the way.

**Register:** SELF-EFFACING — calm, refined, quietly competent. A little character is welcome; loud is not. The feeling it leaves: "this is solid and professional," with nothing demanding to be noticed.

> **This is a baseline, not the design.** A template sets the starting register, palette, and move-set; the actual design is expected to diverge from it wherever the user's brief, content, or audience demands. See "Baselines, not cages" in SKILL.md.

## Pull it when

- Corporate and product marketing sites
- Dashboards and internal tools
- Reports, docs, and data-heavy pages
- Trust-forward B2B and fintech
- Anything that must read credible, then get out of the way

**The test:** if the design should be *noticed*, this isn't it; if it should be *trusted*, it is.

## Wrong choice for

- A consumer brand that needs to shout
- An expressive editorial spread or poster
- A playful, toy-like, or children's product
- Luxury-maximalist or high-fashion identity (→ `ledger.md` for hushed luxury, `prism.md` for exuberant)
- Anything whose whole job is to be memorable-loud

## The vocabulary — type

- **Display + text:** IBM Plex Sans
- **Eyebrows, indices, data:** IBM Plex Mono
- Tabular figures wherever numbers align; measure ~62ch.

## The vocabulary — structure (the fixed move-set)

- **Hairline rules** holding the page true; three depth planes (recessed well / paper ground / raised panel)
- **Mono eyebrows** + **leading-zero section indices**; two-level numbering (Roman chapters / arabic sections)
- **The meridian hairline** — one fine line with a single bearing/degree tick, in one or two spots page-wide. It is the *only* note of character (a maritime **whisper**, never a costume, never the driver)
- A 4-movement chapter spine when the page is long; coordinate-style section headers (eyebrow left, index right)

## Held constants (the core — what must survive for it to still be Meridian)

Off-white ground · hairline order (rules, mono eyebrows, indices) · ONE reserved accent spent only at decisions · the self-effacing register. Everything else — faces, the meridian whisper, the movement spine, even the accent's hue (a brand colour may take the slot) — is swappable within the floors.

## Discipline

- One pop, spent once, live — everywhere else it may appear only as a labeled "spent once" documentation chip.
- All four floors hold (AA computed, chart honesty, legibility, focus/44px targets). The look bends to the rule, always.
