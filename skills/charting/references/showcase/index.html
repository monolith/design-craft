Showcase — richer, library-backed visuals

A deliberate departure from the zero-dependency Tufte gallery.

The main gallery is zero-dependency, flat-SVG, and data-ink-minimal on purpose. This page goes the other way: it leans on three small vendored libraries — three.js + OrbitControls and force-graph — to build six interactive, visually rich scenes.

The discipline that survives the move: 3D is used only where the data is genuinely spatial or relational — a globe, a point cloud, a height field — never as decoration draped over data that is really 2D. Where a flat form reads better (the network, the sunburst, the run timeline), it stays 2D.

Everything loads locally. The three libraries are vendored under lib/; the page fetches nothing over the network at runtime.

1 · Trade-flow globe

Real country borders (Natural Earth 110m) on a calm sphere; ~30 economies tinted by net trade balance on a diverging scale, with thin great-circle arcs for synthetic bilateral flows.

drag to orbit · scroll to zoom · click a tinted country to focus its flows and partners · click empty space to release

2 · 3D scatter — three clusters

~300 points in three Gaussian clusters; axes labelled, genuinely three-dimensional.

drag to orbit · scroll to zoom · clusters separate as you turn it

3 · 3D surface — a height field

z = f(x, y) over a 48×48 grid; height and lighting both shade the relief.

drag to orbit · scroll to zoom

4 · File relationship network

An Obsidian-style force-directed graph on a dark panel — a static snapshot of a real markdown workspace; nodes are files, edges are references, colour is directory, size is in-degree.

hover a node to light up its neighbourhood and dim the rest · drag nodes · scroll to zoom

5 · Radial sunburst

A nested directory tree; ring = depth, angle = size. Hand-built SVG — flat on purpose.

hover a wedge to isolate its branch and read its size

6 · Agent run timeline

A token-consumption timeline (synthetic data): log-y, area-proportional dots by provider, pulsing heartbeats with worker connectors + traveling slivers, a trend ribbon, hard-kill ceiling, mode ribbon (active vs hibernating), and a rejection rug.

hover for the crosshair, family highlight, and details · the heartbeats breathe and the slivers travel on their own
