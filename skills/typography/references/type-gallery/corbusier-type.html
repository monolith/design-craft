Corbusier type — three fonts, three grounds

A single recommended 3-font kit that is modern, easy to read, sits well with Tufte data-ink (real tabular figures, quiet labels, direct labels), and harmonises with the Corbusier palette — shown in full pages with tables and charts across the three preferred Corbusier themes: Light (limestone), Dark (charcoal), Watercolour (white matt). Two alternative trios follow.

The pick — IBM Plex Serif · Sans · Mono

One superfamily, three jobs, guaranteed harmony. Plex is a modernist-engineered system (Bauhaus lineage, "neutral but warm") and IBM Carbon's own data-viz typeface — so it ships tabular figures + a slashed zero, reads cleanly at small sizes, and its restrained, architectural character is a natural fit for Corbusier's muted earth tones. Serif = headings/voice · Sans = body, UI & chart labels · Mono = numbers, code, aligned data.

Two alternative trios — same brief, different voice

Alternative A — Source Serif 4 · Source Sans 3 · JetBrains Mono

Adobe's open superfamily for display + body, with JetBrains Mono for data. Slightly warmer, more editorial serif than Plex; equally screen-clean and free. Pick this if you want a touch more literary voice in headings while keeping data crisp.

Alternative B — Fraunces · Inter · IBM Plex Mono

A cross-family trio with more personality: Fraunces is a warm, high-contrast display serif (real character in headings); Inter keeps the body neutral and screen-clean; Plex Mono carries the data. Higher voice, slightly higher risk — pick this when the brief asks for brand warmth on top of the Corbusier ground.
