The quiet drift of the standard

A garment labelled "size 8" has quietly grown for decades. The number on the tag stayed put; the tape measure did not.

The story of that drift is, in miniature, the story of how a standard erodes when no one in particular is accountable for keeping it. It begins, as these things often do, with a well-meaning compromise — and ends with a number that means almost nothing at all.

Each individual adjustment was defensible. A label nudged half a centimetre to flatter; a competitor matched; a decade passed. No single decision broke it — the absence of anyone defending it did.

By Maren Holt · Issue 14 · 2026
