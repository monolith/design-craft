# Brand Direction — template

The output of an absorption. Fill it from the **artifacts**, not from memory. Keep it to ~one page of
substance plus attached evidence. Delete rows that don't apply; never pad with the generic floor.

> **PRECEDENCE (state this at the top, verbatim intent):** This Brand Direction is *binding*. For this
> work it **overrides design-craft's own defaults and house look** — `visual-style` yields, and the
> colour, type, layout, and composition skills serve the values below. Deviations are a `branding`
> decision (redesign-risk), not a styling choice.

## The "hand" (the half a colour-picker can't see — required)
Two to four plain sentences on how the brand *moves*: its compositional logic and tone. Editorial-calm or
dense? Restrained or maximal? Earnest or dry? Warm-imperfect or precise? This is what must survive a
layout change. *If this box is empty, the absorption isn't done.*

## Colour
| Hex | Role (confirmed) | Coverage | Do | Don't / "this is them, not us" |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| `#______` | hero / ink / surface / accent | from `extract_palette.py` | how it's used (e.g. full-bleed planes) | the house-style trap to avoid (e.g. "don't demote the hero to a timid accent or add our accent colours") |

Hand off exact values, contrast pairs, and CVD checks to **graphical-perception-and-color**.

## Type
- **Display / headline:** ‹face, weight, how big "big" is› — *do* … / *don't* (the "them not us" trap).
- **Text / UI:** ‹face, size, measure, numerals›.
- Recording what they use; face *selection* is **typography**'s job.

## Logo & space
Construction, lockups, clear-space, and the hard "never": ‹never recolour / restretch / reset in a system
font / crop›. Attach the lockup.

## Imagery
Illustration vs photography; treatment (texture, duotone, halftone, "made by a person" vs slick stock);
how colour lives inside it. *Do* … / *don't* (usually the easiest signature to lose — protect it hardest).

## Voice & tone
Register in one line + 3 real sample phrases pulled from their copy. Link their content style guide if one
exists.

## Motion *(only if the brand has one)*
Easing, pace, signature transitions. Omit if there's nothing distinctive.

## Non-negotiables — the must-survive list
The 3–6 signals that, if dropped, stop it reading as them. (This is the absorption's headline.)

## Deviation latitude → hand to `branding`
How committed is the audience, and how far can the refresh move before it costs equity? This is a
**branding** redesign-risk call (Walsh et al. 2010), not a styling one — flag it, don't decide it here.

## 30-second fit test
Hold a candidate screen against this page: hero colour present and dominant? display face right? imagery
in their treatment? voice matches? the "hand" intact? If any "no," it's drifting toward house style.
